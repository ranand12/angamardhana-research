Angamardana (Full Body Conditioning)

A. Standing Postures
1. Calf Raises (V-Stance)
Stand with your heels touching and toes pointing outward in a V-shape.

Breath pattern : Inhale as you go up stay for few deep breaths and exhale as you come down.

Posture 1: Point your arms straight up alongside your ears. Lift your heels off the ground. Balance on your toes. Lower your heels.

Posture 2: Interlace your fingers and place your hands firmly behind your neck/ears. Lift your heels. Balance. Lower your heels.

Posture 3: Reach your hands behind your back and grab your forearms (criss-cross). Lift your heels. Balance. Lower your heels.

Posture 4: Interlace your fingers and press your palms up toward the ceiling. Lift your heels. Balance. Lower your heels. Release your hands.

2. Utthita Lolasana Variation (Forward Folds / Swinging While Standing - V-Stance)
Stand with your heels touching and toes pointing outward in a V-shape.

Breath pattern : Exhale as you go down and then do 3 forced exhales from the put of your throat with a mild constrictions and then inhale as you bring your torso up

Posture 1: Point your arms straight up alongside your ears. Fold your torso forward and down. Stand back up straight. 

Posture 2: Interlace your fingers behind your neck/ears. Fold your torso forward and down. Stand back up straight.

Posture 3: Criss-cross your arms behind your back. Fold your torso forward and down. Stand back up straight.

Posture 4: Interlace your fingers, press your palms up toward the ceiling. Fold your torso forward and down. Stand back up straight. Release hands.

3. Crossed Legs (Right over Left)

Cross your right leg tightly in front of your left leg. Both feet should be flat if possible. Right foot should be next to left foot but crossed over 

Breath pattern : Exhale as you go down and then do 3 forced exhales from the put of your throat with a mild constrictions and then inhale as you bring your torso up

Posture 1: Arms straight up alongside your ears. Fold your torso forward. Stand up.

Posture 2: Interlace fingers behind your neck/ears. Fold your torso forward. Stand up.

Posture 3: Criss-cross arms behind your back. Fold your torso forward. Stand up.

Posture 4: Interlace fingers, press palms up to the ceiling. Fold your torso forward. Stand up. Release hands.

4. Crossed Legs (Left over Right)
Cross your left  leg tightly in front of your right leg. Both feet should be flat if possible. Right foot should be next to left foot but crossed over 

Breath pattern : Exhale as you go down and then do 3 forced exhales from the put of your throat with a mild constrictions and then inhale as you bring your torso up

Posture 1: Arms straight up alongside your ears. Fold your torso forward. Stand up.

Posture 2: Interlace fingers behind your neck/ears. Fold your torso forward. Stand up.

Posture 3: Criss-cross arms behind your back. Fold your torso forward. Stand up.

Posture 4: Interlace fingers, press palms up to the ceiling. Fold your torso forward. Stand up. Release hands.

5. Parsvottanasana / Intense Side Stretch Pose (Right Side)
Take a 3 foot step out with your right foot. Your left foot faces forward; your right foot points to the right side. Turn your entire torso to face the right side. Keep both legs straight with the front knee fully extended throughout.

Breath pattern : Exhale as you go down and then do 3 forced exhales from the put of your throat with a mild constrictions and then inhale as you bring your torso up

Posture 1: Arms straight up alongside your ears. Fold your torso down over your right leg. Stand back up.

Posture 2: Interlace fingers behind your neck/ears. Fold your torso down over your right leg. Stand back up.

Posture 3: Criss-cross arms behind your back. Fold your torso down over your right leg. Stand back up.

Posture 4 (Parvatasana arms / overhead interlocked-finger stretch): Interlace fingers, press palms up to the ceiling. Fold your torso down over your right leg. Stand back up.

Posture 5 (Cow Face / Gomukhasana arms): Reach your right arm up and drop your hand down your back. Reach your left arm behind you and grab your right fingers (use a towel if they don't touch). Fold your torso down over your right leg. Stand back up.

Posture 6 (Reverse Prayer / Paschima Namaskarasana): Place your hands behind your back and press your palms together in a prayer position. Fold your torso down over your right leg. Stand back up. Release hands.

6. Parsvottanasana / Intense Side Stretch Pose (Left Side)

Take a 3 foot step out with your left foot. Your right foot faces forward; your left foot points to the right side. Turn your entire torso to face the left side. Keep both legs straight with the front knee fully extended throughout.

Breath pattern : Exhale as you go down and then do 3 forced exhales from the put of your throat with a mild constrictions and then inhale as you bring your torso up

Posture 1: Arms straight up alongside your ears. Fold your torso down over your left leg. Stand back up.

Posture 2: Interlace fingers behind your neck/ears. Fold your torso down over your left leg. Stand back up.

Posture 3: Criss-cross arms behind your back. Fold your torso down over your left leg. Stand back up.

Posture 4 (Parvatasana arms / overhead interlocked-finger stretch): Interlace fingers, press palms up to the ceiling. Fold your torso down over your left leg. Stand back up.

Posture 5 (Cow Face / Gomukhasana arms): Reach your left arm up and drop your hand down your back. Reach your right arm behind you and grab your left fingers. Fold your torso down over your left leg. Stand back up.

Posture 6 (Reverse Prayer / Paschima Namaskarasana): Place your hands behind your back and press your palms together in a prayer position. Fold your torso down over your left leg. Stand back up. Release hands and step feet back together.

7. Tiryaka Tadasana (Swaying Palm Tree Pose / Lateral Pulses - Right Side)

B. Squatting Postures
1. Front Leg Extensions
Drop down into a low squat, balancing on your toes/balls of your feet. Keep your back straight. Gaze straight ahead.

Balance in your fingers not palms

Breath pattern : Exhale as you extend your leg hold and breath normally for 3-5 seconds inhale and bring leg back to original position 

Right Leg: Exhale and extend your right leg perfectly straight out in front of you. Keep it off the floor. Hold for 3 seconds. Pull it back into the squat. Repeat thrice

Left Leg: Exhale and extend your left leg perfectly straight out in front of you. Keep it off the floorHold for 3 seconds. Pull it back. Repeat thrice


2. Side Leg Extensions
Drop down into a low squat, balancing on your toes/balls of your feet. Keep your back straight. Gaze straight ahead.

Breath pattern : Exhale as you extend your leg hold and breath normally for 3-5 seconds inhale and bring leg back to original position 

Right Leg: Keep your back straight. Exhale and extend your right leg perfectly straight out to the right side. Keep it off the floor. Pull it back. Repeat thrice


Do the same for left leg

3. Skandasana Variation (Side-Lunge Hip Lifts)

Squat on both toes. Extend your right leg straight out to the right side and leave it there. Right leg and foot on the floor left foot in squat. Place fingers from both hands flat on the floor directly in front of you.

Exhale, straighten your left leg to push your hips high up into the air, and bring your nose down toward your right knee.

Inhale and Drop your hips back down to the starting position.

Repeat thrice 

Do the same for left leg.

4. Squat Jumps
Starting position squat with palms on ground and toe on ground. Palms in front of foot. Inhale and Pull both legs back into the low squat. Arch your spine as you bring your foot back. Keep your palms flat on the floor in front of you. 

Set 1: Take one deep breath in. Without inhaling again, jump your feet to the left, jump to the middle, jump to the right.

Set 2: Deep breath in. Jump left, jump middle, jump right.

Set 3: Deep breath in. Jump left, jump middle, jump right.

5. Breathless Push-ups

From the squat, inhale deeply. Exhale and jump your feet back so you are in a high plank (push-up position).

Perform as many push-ups as you can without taking a single breath.

The exact second you need to breathe, immediately drop your knees to the floor and push your hips back into Child's Pose.

C. Sitting Postures


1. Dandasana Variation (L-Sit Single-Leg Lifts)

Sit fully flat on the floor with your legs extended straight out in front of you (making a 90-degree L-shape). Keep your feet flexed (toes pointing up) and place your hands flat on the floor next to your hips. Keep your back perfectly straight.

Right Leg: Inhale and lift your straight right leg up off the floor as high as you can without leaning backward. Release it down. Lift again. Release. Lift a third time. Release.

Left Leg: Inhale and lift your straight left leg up off the floor as high as you can. Release it down. Lift again. Release. Lift a third time. Release.

2. Naukasana (Boat Pose)

Rep 1: Lift both straight legs into the air (if too hard, bend your knees) and balance on your tailbone/sit bones with your torso leaning slightly back. Hold this V-shape. Lower your legs down.

Rep 2: Lift both legs into the air and balance. Hold. Lower.

Rep 3: Lift both legs into the air and balance. Hold. Lower.

3. Ubhaya Padangusthasana (Both Big Toes Pose / Toe Pulls)

in a seated position Grab your big toes with the first two fingers of each hand.

Rep 1: Inhale, lift both legs into the air. Keep your spine perfectly straight. Bend your elbows outward to actively pull your legs closer to your chest. Lower legs down.

Rep 2: Lift legs, keep spine straight, bend elbows and pull. Lower legs.

Rep 3: Lift legs, keep spine straight, bend elbows and pull. Lower legs.

4. Parsva Upavistha Konasana (Side Seated Wide-Angle Pose - Left Side)



Sit down and Spread both legs out wide into a V-shape. Turn your shoulders to face your left leg.

Posture 1: Arms straight up alongside your ears. Fold forward over your left leg. Sit up.

Posture 2: Interlace fingers behind your neck/ears. Fold over your left leg. Sit up.

Posture 3: Criss-cross arms behind your back. Fold over your left leg. Sit up.

Posture 4 (Parvatasana arms / overhead interlocked-finger stretch): Interlace fingers, press palms to the ceiling. Fold over your left leg. Sit up.

Posture 5 (Cow Face / Gomukhasana arms): Reach left arm up and down your back. Reach right arm behind to grab left fingers. Fold over left leg. Sit up.

Posture 6 (Reverse Prayer / Paschima Namaskarasana): Hands in prayer behind your back. Fold over left leg. Sit up.

5. Parsva Upavistha Konasana (Side Seated Wide-Angle Pose - Right Side)
Stay in the wide V-shape. Turn your shoulders to face your right leg.

Posture 1: Arms straight up alongside your ears. Fold forward over your right leg. Sit up.

Posture 2: Interlace fingers behind your neck/ears. Fold over your right leg. Sit up.

Posture 3: Criss-cross arms behind your back. Fold over your right leg. Sit up.

Posture 4 (Parvatasana arms / overhead interlocked-finger stretch): Interlace fingers, press palms to the ceiling. Fold over your right leg. Sit up.

Posture 5 (Cow Face / Gomukhasana arms): Reach right arm up and down your back. Reach left arm behind to grab right fingers. Fold over right leg. Sit up.

Posture 6 (Reverse Prayer / Paschima Namaskarasana): Hands in prayer behind your back. Fold over right leg. Sit up.

6. Virasana / Vajrasana (Seated Rest)

Bring your legs together. Roll onto your knees.

Either sit directly on your heels (knees together), or separate your feet slightly and lower your hips entirely to the floor between your heels.

Rest your hands loosely on your thighs. Pull your shoulders back and down. Keep your chin up.

Close your eyes and focus on the space between your eyebrows. Rest here, breathing naturally for a few minutes.

To exit: Place your palms on the floor, lift your hips up, and swing your legs out from underneath you.

D. Lying Down Postures
Lie totally flat on your back. Keep your heels together and arms by your sides.

1. Supta Pawanmuktasana (Knee to Chest / Leg Lock Pose)

Right Side (Rep 1): Bend your right knee and use your hands to pull it tightly against your chest. Lift your head off the mat and try to touch your nose to your knee. Release head and leg back to the floor.

Right Side (Rep 2): Pull right knee to chest, lift nose to knee. Release.

Right Side (Rep 3): Pull right knee to chest, lift nose to knee. Release.

Left Side (Rep 1): Bend your left knee, pull it to your chest, lift nose to knee. Release.

Left Side (Rep 2): Pull left knee to chest, lift nose to knee. Release.

Left Side (Rep 3): Pull left knee to chest, lift nose to knee. Release.

2. Jhulana Lurhakanasana (Rocking)

Sit in the squatting position with the buttocks just above the floor. Interlock the fingers of both hands and clasp them around the shins just below the knees. Rock the whole body backwards and forwards on the spine. Try to come up into the squatting pose on the feet when rocking forward. Practise 3 to 5 backward and forward movements.

Breathing: Inhale as you roll backwards. Exhale as you come forward.

Practice note: Use a folded blanket for this practice so that there is no possibility of causing damage to the spine. While rocking back, the head should remain forward. Be careful not to hit the head on the floor.

3. Jathara Parivartanasana (Revolved Abdomen Pose — bent-knee/chair-leg variation)

Lie flat on your back. Lift your legs so the hips and knees are bent at 90 degrees ("chair legs" / tabletop). Spread your arms out wide at shoulder level for support.

Exhale and lower both legs together to one side (keeping the knees bent and stacked, close to the floor but not resting). Inhale and bring the legs back to the centre. Repeat to the other side.

Breathing: Exhale while lowering the legs to the side. Inhale while returning to centre.

4. Core Sweeps (leg-circle / Chakra Padasana-style)

Rep 1: Inhale and bring both knees to your chest. Exhale and push both legs straight out so they are hovering at a 45-degree angle. Keeping them straight, bring them back to chest. Then sweep them out to the right side, close to the ground but not on the ground should be off the ground. Then bring it back closer to ground and then to chest and then 45 degree angle and then repeat left side and done. 

Rep 2: Knees to chest. Push out to 45 degrees. Sweep to the right, lift straight up to 90 degrees, lower to the mat.

Rep 3: Knees to chest. Push out to 45 degrees. Sweep to the right, lift straight up to 90 degrees, lower to the mat.

5. Supta Udarakarshanasana (Spinal Twists / Sleeping Abdominal Stretch Pose)
(Also a combination of Supta Udarakarshanasana and Jathara Parivartanasana — supine spinal twist)

Bend both knees so your feet are flat on the mat. Spread your arms out wide into a "T" shape, palms facing up.

Twist 1: Drop both knees down to the floor on your left side. Turn your head to look at your right hand.

Twist 2: Lift knees back to center. Drop both knees to the floor on your right side. Turn your head to look at your left hand.

Twist 3: Lift knees back to center. Drop to the left side again.

Twist 4: Lift knees back to center. Drop to the right side again.

Twist 5: Lift knees back to center. Drop to the left side again.

Twist 6: Lift knees back to center. Drop to the right side again. Return to center.

6. Yoga Bicycle (Dwichakrikasana-style cycling)

Place your hands behind your head and lift your feet off the floor.

For exactly 10 seconds, do a rapid, aggressive pedaling motion with your legs.

As you pedal, twist your torso so your right elbow firmly strikes your left knee, then your left elbow strikes your right knee. Ensure the non-striking elbow taps the floor on every rep. The leg should not rest on floor. 

Go as fast as physically possible for as much as possible

Part 5: Balancing & Savasana
1. Warrior III Balance

Roll over to your right side and push yourself up to a standing position.

Step your left foot forward.

Inhale and reach both arms straight up by your ears.

Exhale, engage your core, and lean your torso forward until your upper body is parallel to the ground. Simultaneously lift your straight right leg up behind you until it is also parallel to the ground.

Bring your hands together in prayer at your forehead.

Pull your prayer hands down to the center of your chest.

Push your prayer hands back out to your forehead, then extend them straight out in front of you.

Inhale, lift your chest, and place your right foot back down.

Switch legs: Step right foot forward. Inhale arms up. Exhale lean forward, lift left leg up. Bring hands in prayer to forehead. Pull to chest. Push to forehead. Extend arms straight. Step back down.

2. Savasana (Corpse Pose)

Lie completely flat on your back.

Separate your legs slightly so they naturally flop open. Let your arms rest about a foot away from your body with the palms facing the ceiling.

Active physical release: Wiggle your toes. Rotate your ankles in both directions. Squeeze your hands into tight fists, then let them fall completely open. Slowly turn your head left, right, and back to center.

Close your eyes and lay perfectly still for 5 minutes. Do not move a single muscle. Let any thoughts that arise pass by without interacting with them.

To wake up: After 5 minutes, take a deep inhale. Slowly bend your knees toward your chest. Roll entirely onto your right side and rest there for a few seconds. Plant your hands and slowly push yourself up to a seated position.
